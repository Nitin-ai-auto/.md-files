### What’s Included in Your Wyoming Paycheck?

Though Wyoming doesn't levy a state income tax, your paycheck will still have standard federal deductions. Otto AI Wyoming tax calculator considers the following:

*   **Federal Income Tax Withholding**

This depends on your gross income, filing status (e.g., Single, Married Filing Jointly), number of dependents, and withholding preferences on Form W-4. Federal tax brackets range from 10% to 37%, and your tax is calculated progressively.
*   **FICA Taxes: Social Security & Medicare**

All employees in Wyoming contribute: 6.2% of wages to Social Security (up to the wage base limit) and 1.45% to Medicare, with an extra 0.9% for income over $200,000. These taxes are required by federal law and appear on every paycheck.
*   **Pre-Tax Contributions**

These deductions reduce your taxable income and might include: 401(k)/403(b) retirement contributions, Health Savings Accounts (HSA), Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA), and employer-provided insurance premiums.
