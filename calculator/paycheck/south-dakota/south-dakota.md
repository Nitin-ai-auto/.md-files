### What Affects Your Paycheck in South Dakota?

Although South Dakota doesn't charge state income tax, you'll still see deductions on your paycheck for federal obligations and other withholdings. Otto AI paycheck calculator South Dakota takes all of these into account for a complete picture of your take-home pay.

*   **Federal Income Tax**

Federal income taxes are based on IRS tax brackets, which range from 10% to 37% depending on your income and filing status. Otto AI automatically applies the most current brackets and considers your W-4 allowances to accurately calculate your tax liability.
*   **FICA Taxes**

Every paycheck includes deductions for: Social Security: 6.2% of your wages up to the IRS wage base limit; Medicare: 1.45% of your wages; Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% for individuals earning over $200,000. These mandatory federal taxes are applied to all income and are reflected in every pay period.
*   **Pre-Tax Deductions**

If you contribute to employer-sponsored plans, your taxable income is lowered, which means less tax is withheld and more net pay for you. These include: 401(k)/403(b) retirement plans; Health Savings Account (HSA); Flexible Spending Account (FSA); Employer-sponsored insurance premiums. Otto AI South Dakota take home pay calculator accounts for all your pre-tax contributions to provide a true estimate of your post-tax pay.
