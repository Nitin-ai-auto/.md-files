### Breaking Down Your New Jersey Paycheck

New Jersey residents face both federal and state-level deductions that can significantly affect their net income. Otto AI makes it easy to visualize these deductions so you can budget with confidence. Here's what typically comes out of your paycheck:

*   **Federal Income Tax:** Based on income level, filing status, and claimed withholdings.
*   **FICA Taxes:** 6.2% for Social Security and 1.45% for Medicare. An additional 0.9% Medicare tax may apply to high earners.
*   **New Jersey State Income Tax:** Rates range from 1.4% to 10.75%, depending on your income.
*   **State Disability Insurance (SDI):** Required in NJ to fund temporary disability insurance.
*   **Family Leave Insurance (FLI):** Another mandatory state deduction that helps cover family leave.
*   **Pre-Tax Contributions:** Contributions to retirement plans (like a 401(k)), health savings accounts (HSA), or flexible spending accounts (FSA), and employer-sponsored health insurance.
