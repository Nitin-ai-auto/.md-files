Why Shortcuts Matter in ZBrush

Essential Keyboard Shortcuts to Know
------------------------------------

ZBrush has a wide range of shortcuts designed to help you access tools and perform tasks rapidly. Here are some of the most fundamental shortcuts every ZBrush user should memorize:

### General Shortcuts

*   Ctrl + N: Create a new document.
*   Ctrl + S: Save your work.
*   Ctrl + Z: Undo the last action.
*   Ctrl + Shift + Z: Redo the last action.

### Navigation Shortcuts

*   Alt + Click: Temporarily switch to the selection tool.
*   Space Bar: Activate the Move tool temporarily.
*   Shift + F: Toggle the Poly F (Polyframe) mode on/off.

### Sculpting Tool Shortcuts

*   B: Access the Brush Palette.
*   M: Access the Material Palette.
*   T: Toggle Edit mode.

### Working with Subtools

*   N: Select the next Subtool.
*   Shift + N: Select the previous Subtool.
*   Ctrl + Shift + D: Duplicate the selected Subtool.

Advanced Shortcuts for Professionals
------------------------------------

As you become more comfortable with ZBrush, implementing advanced shortcuts can further enhance your productivity. Here are a few tricks to consider:

### Quick Access Shortcuts

*   Alt + Drag: Masking any area to quickly isolate sections of your model.
*   Shift + G: Toggle the gizmo on/off to manipulate your model with precision.

### Custom Shortcuts

ZBrush allows you to customize your own shortcuts tailored to your workflow:

1.   Go to Preferences.
2.   Select Hotkeys.
3.   From here, you can assign new shortcuts to your favorite functions or tools.

Organizing Your Workspace
-------------------------

A well-organized workspace can also improve your efficiency in ZBrush. Consider the following tips:

*   Set Up Custom Palettes: Group relevant tools in custom palettes for easier access.
*   Utilize the Shortcut Bar: Pin your most-used tools for quick navigation.
*   Color Code Your Tools: This can help differentiate between similar tools visually.

Best Practices for Learning Shortcuts
-------------------------------------

*   Regular Practice: Dedicate time each session to practice using shortcuts until they become second nature.
*   Keep a Reference Guide: Print out a list of shortcuts or keep an easy-access list on your desktop.
*   Watch Tutorials: Look for video tutorials focusing on ZBrush shortcuts.
