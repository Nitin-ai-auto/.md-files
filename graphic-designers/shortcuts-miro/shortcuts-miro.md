What is Miro?
-------------

Before diving into shortcuts, let’s briefly discuss Miro. Launched in 2011, Miro allows users to create and collaborate on digital whiteboards in real-time. With its suite of tools, users can draw, write, add notes, and integrate with other software tools, making it ideal for teams looking to enhance creativity and collaboration.

Why Use Shortcuts in Miro?
--------------------------

The primary advantage of using shortcuts in Miro is the ability to perform tasks quickly without fumbling through menus. Shortcuts can save precious seconds that add up during a busy work session. They enhance fluidity, allowing teams to focus on ideation rather than navigation.

Essential Miro Shortcuts You Should Know
----------------------------------------

### Navigation Shortcuts

*    Zoom In/Out: 
    *   Windows: Ctrl + Mouse Scroll
    *   Mac: Command + Mouse Scroll

 Quickly adjust your view with these handy shortcuts to see the big picture or focus closely on details. 
*    Pan the Board: 
    *   Windows: Space + Click and drag
    *   Mac: Space + Click and drag

 Easily move around large boards without losing your place. 

### Creation Shortcuts

*    Create a New Board: 
    *   Windows: Ctrl + N
    *   Mac: Command + N

 Instantly start a new project. 
*    Add a Sticky Note: 
    *   Windows: N
    *   Mac: N

 Get your ideas down faster with a simple keystroke. 

### Text and Shapes

*    Text Tool: 
    *   Windows: T
    *   Mac: T

 Quickly switch to the text tool to add notes and annotations to your board. 
*    Shape Tool: 
    *   Windows: R (for rectangle), O (for circle), etc.
    *   Mac: R, O

 Use these to draw shapes without taking your hands off the keyboard. 

### Command Shortcuts

*    Undo/Redo: 
    *   Windows: Ctrl + Z / Ctrl + Y
    *   Mac: Command + Z / Command + Y

 Fix mistakes quickly and efficiently. 
*    Group/Ungroup Objects: 
    *   Windows: Ctrl + G / Ctrl + Shift + G
    *   Mac: Command + G / Command + Shift + G

 Manage clustered elements with ease. 

Customizing Your Shortcuts
--------------------------

Miro allows users to customize shortcuts to suit their needs, making it a flexible tool tailored to individual workflows. To do this, go to Account Settings > Shortcuts. Here, you can assign your preferred keystrokes to streamline your own unique work process.
