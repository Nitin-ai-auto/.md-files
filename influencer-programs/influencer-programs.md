Check how the brand’s product range aligns with your niche, review previous collaborator content if available, and consider whether the campaign style will resonate with your followers.

### What criteria should I use to evaluate an influencer programme?



Key factors include commission rate or compensation structure, product relevance, brand reputation, creative freedom allowed, disclosure and compliance terms, and tracking support for your referrals or links.

### Are there follower minimums or other eligibility requirements for these programmes?



It varies by brand and programme; some require a certain follower count, specific engagement levels or particular content formats, while others accept micro-influencers and niche creators—so check each programme’s terms.

### How do content creators typically join one of these programmes?



The process usually involves registering your social media channels or blog URL, submitting audience metrics or media kit, receiving approval, getting a unique affiliate or referral link, and then creating content according to the campaign brief.

### What kinds of content work best when promoting brand influencer/affiliate programmes?



Authentic, value-driven content tends to perform well, e.g., tutorials, unboxings, reviews, lifestyle integrations, “how I use this product” posts. The key is aligning the product with your personal brand and making it useful for your audience.

### How is performance tracked and commissions paid?



Most programmes provide a dashboard or portal where you can monitor clicks, conversions or referral sales. After meeting any minimum-payout threshold, you’ll receive payment according to the programme’s payment cycle.

### What should I watch out for before joining an influencer programme?



Important considerations include: exclusivity clauses that restrict you from working with competing brands, unclear commission terms, lack of product relevance for your audience, low brand recognition, or rigid content rules that don’t fit your style.
