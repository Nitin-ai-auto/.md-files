Equipment and Technology Expenses
---------------------------------

Photography Gear, Lighting, Computers, Software, and Props. Section 179 and depreciation rules apply.

Camera Bodies and Lenses

Capital expenses like cameras and lenses ($2,000-$10,000+) can be deducted through depreciation over their useful life (typically 5-7 years) or potentially expensed in full using Section 179 deduction, which allows up to $1,160,000 in qualifying equipment to be expensed in 2023. Keep records of purchase dates, cost, and business use percentage.

$

Lighting Equipment

Studio lights, flashes, strobes, reflectors, diffusers, and other lighting accessories are deductible business expenses. They can either be depreciated or expensed in full through Section 179 if they qualify.

$

Computers and Software

Computers, tablets, and software used for photography business are deductible. Montana follows federal rules for Section 179 and bonus depreciation.

$

Props and Backdrops

Studio props, backdrops, and sets are deductible business expenses.

$

Drones

Drones used for aerial photography are deductible business equipment. FAA registration and licensing fees are also deductible.

$

Studio and Office Expenses
--------------------------

Rent, Utilities, Insurance, and Home Office Deductions.

Studio Rent

Rent paid for studio space is fully deductible as a business expense.

$

Home Office Deduction

If part of your home is used regularly and exclusively for business, you can deduct a portion of your housing costs. Montana follows IRS guidelines for home office deductions using Form 8829.

$

Utilities

Electricity, water, internet, and phone services for your studio or business portion of your home are deductible.

$

Insurance

Business liability insurance, equipment insurance, and a portion of homeowner's insurance (if using a home office) are deductible.

$

Office Supplies

Paper, ink, stationery, and other consumable office supplies are fully deductible in the year purchased.

$

Travel and Transportation
-------------------------

Vehicle Expenses, Travel Costs, and Mileage Deductions.

Mileage

Business-related driving can be deducted using the standard mileage rate (65.5 cents per mile for 2023) or actual expenses method. Keep a detailed mileage log.

$

Vehicle Expenses

If using the actual expenses method, you can deduct a percentage of gas, maintenance, insurance, and depreciation based on business use percentage.

$

Parking and Tolls

Business-related parking fees and tolls are fully deductible, regardless of whether you use the standard mileage rate or actual expenses method.

$

Travel to Shoots

Airfare, lodging, and 50% of meals while traveling for business purposes are deductible.

$

Car Rentals

Rental cars used for business purposes during travel are deductible.

$

Professional Services and Fees
------------------------------

Accounting, Legal, Banking, and Contractor Payments.

Accounting Services

Fees paid to accountants or bookkeepers for tax preparation, bookkeeping, or financial advice are deductible.

$

Legal Services

Attorney fees for contract reviews, business formation, or copyright registration are deductible.

$

Professional Memberships

Dues for professional photography associations are deductible.

$

Banking Fees and Payment Processing

Business account fees, credit card processing charges, and payment platform fees (like PayPal) are deductible business expenses.

$

Subcontractor and Assistant Fees

Payments to second shooters, assistants, or editors are deductible. For payments exceeding $600 annually to any individual, a Form 1099-NEC must be issued.

$

Tax Compliance and Planning
---------------------------

Montana State Tax Considerations and Filing Requirements.

Montana Business Equipment Tax

Montana imposes a business equipment tax on business personal property. The first $100,000 of business equipment is exempt from taxation.

$

Sales Tax Considerations

Montana does not have a state sales tax, which is advantageous for photographers selling physical products.

$

Quarterly Estimated Taxes

Photographers with tax liabilities over $1,000 must file quarterly estimated tax payments using Form 1040-ES.

$

Montana Tax Credits

Check for available Montana tax credits that may apply to your photography business, such as credits for certain types of investments or energy-efficient improvements.

$

Documentation Requirements

Maintain organized records of all expenses, receipts, and business transactions for at least 7 years to support your tax deductions in case of an audit.
