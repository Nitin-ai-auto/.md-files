Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera & Equipment Purchases

Deduct gear like cameras, lenses, and computers under Section 179 or depreciate over time. VA follows federal Section 179 limits but disallows bonus depreciation—add it back when filing state taxes. File IRS Form 4562.

$

Equipment Repairs & Maintenance

Repair and maintenance costs like sensor cleaning, tune-ups, and part replacements are fully deductible. Save receipts. These are ordinary business expenses and deductible federally and in Virginia.

$

Equipment Rentals VA

Short-term rentals like cameras and lighting kits are 100% deductible. If leased long-term, you may need to capitalize and depreciate. VA treats rental expenses like federal—sales tax paid is included in the deduction.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities And Insurance
-------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Studio Rent & Utilities

Rent for studios or co-working spaces is fully deductible. Utilities like electricity, internet, and water used solely for business count too. VA conforms to federal treatment—no adjustment needed for state tax.

$

Home Office Deduction

If space is used exclusively for business, deduct a portion of home expenses. Use actual expense or simplified ($5/sq ft) method. VA accepts the federal deduction as-is—no additional state form required.

$

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for liability, equipment, or property insurance are fully deductible. This includes errors & omissions coverage. VA doesn’t tax insurance proceeds and allows full deduction of business-related insurance expenses.

$

Travel & Transportation Expenses
--------------------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Business Travel (Overnight)

Expenses for business travel—airfare, lodging, taxis, baggage fees, and 50% of meals—are deductible. Trips must be primarily for business. Virginia follows federal rules and allows full deduction of these travel costs.

$

Local Vehicle Use

Deduct business driving IRS rate of $0.67/mile or actual costs (gas, insurance, depreciation). Maintain a mileage log. VA adopts federal standards—no special mileage rules or rate differences.

$

Commuting vs. Business Use

Driving from home to a regular workplace is not deductible. But trips from a home office to client sites or between gigs are. VA conforms to federal definitions of commuting vs. deductible business mileage.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contractor Payments

Paying second shooters, editors, or freelance assistants is fully deductible. Use IRS Form 1099-NEC for payments over $600. VA treats these costs the same—fully deductible and not subject to state sales tax.

$

Documentation & Compliance

Collect W-9 forms from contractors. Keep records of services, amounts, and proof of payment. Virginia does not impose additional reporting beyond federal 1099 requirements unless contractor income is sourced within VA.

$

Employee vs. Contractor

Misclassifying workers can trigger penalties. Contractors control their work; employees do not. VA follows IRS rules. If classified as employees, wages and VA unemployment tax are also deductible business expenses.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development VA
-------------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Workshops & Courses

You can deduct fees for photography workshops, webinars, and courses that enhance current skills. VA mirrors federal rules—deductions allowed if the education improves your existing business, not if it qualifies you for a new trade.

$

Books & Subscriptions

Expenses for trade journals, photography books, tutorials, and learning platforms are deductible. These count as continuing education or office expenses. VA accepts these deductions with no adjustments.

$

Certifications & Memberships

Dues for professional groups (e.g. PPA), industry certifications, and seminar registrations are deductible. Virginia follows federal treatment—deduct if clearly tied to business improvement or client-facing credentials.

$

Travel for Training

Travel to attend qualified workshops is deductible under travel expenses. Keep receipts and course details. VA permits the same deduction if the primary trip purpose is business education.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Online Presence
----------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & online presence

Online & Print Advertising

Costs for Google ads, Facebook boosts, flyers, and brochures are fully deductible. Virginia (VA) conforms to federal rules. Advertising services are sales tax–exempt in VA, lowering your in-state expense.

$

Website Expenses

Domain fees, hosting, portfolio subscriptions, SEO services, and web design are deductible marketing expenses. VA treats them the same—no sales tax if classified as advertising services.

$

Promotions & Branding

Costs for logo design, business cards, branded USBs, or expo booth fees are deductible. VA allows full deduction and often exempts printed advertising from sales tax.

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for general liability, E&O, and gear insurance are fully deductible. This includes auto insurance for business use. Virginia (VA) does not tax these benefits and allows full federal-level deduction.

$

Business Licenses & Local Permits

Fees for BPOL licenses, city photography permits, or drone certifications are deductible. In VA, local gross receipts taxes (BPOL) are also deductible on Schedule C as business tax expenses.

$

Entity & State Filing Fees

Annual VA LLC registration ($50), DBA filings, and permit renewals are deductible. These are considered legal or regulatory fees. VA conforms fully to federal treatment—no adjustments needed.

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Consumables & Studio Props

Items like backdrops, fabrics, memory cards, and decor used in shoots are fully deductible. If used in VA, sales tax is included in cost. Items under $2,500 may be expensed under safe harbor.

$

Supplies for Client Deliverables

Paper, albums, and frames used in prints for clients are deductible. In VA, you can use a resale exemption certificate to avoid sales tax when buying goods you later resell to clients.

$

Use Tax & Online Purchases

If you buy business supplies online and weren’t charged VA sales tax, you may owe VA use tax. The use tax you pay is deductible. Track and report if not collected at checkout.

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank & Merchant Fees

Monthly business bank charges, card processing fees (e.g., Stripe, PayPal), and wire fees are deductible. VA accepts these deductions without modification. Keep bank statements for proof.

$

Loan & Credit Card Interest

Interest on business loans or equipment financing is deductible, but not the principal. Credit card interest is deductible if purchases are business-related. VA conforms to federal rules and allows full deduction.

$

Financial Services & Payment Charges

Accountant fees, bookkeeping costs, and financial consulting related to your business are deductible. Also deductible: credit card annual fees and online payment processing costs. VA mirrors federal treatment without adjustments.

$

Other Deductions & VA-Specific Perks
------------------------------------

Deductions for other expenses and VA-specific perks

Business Meals

You can deduct 50% of meals with clients or during travel, as long as there is a clear business purpose. VA follows federal rules—no extra state limits. Keep itemized receipts and purpose notes.

$

Mixed-Use Assets (Phones, Cameras)

Deduct the business-use portion of personal items like phones or cameras. For example, if 70% used for business, deduct 70% of the cost. VA follows federal allocation—track your usage and save logs.

$

Uniforms & Branded Gear

Logo shirts, safety gear, or special attire not for everyday wear are deductible. Dry cleaning for regular clothing is not. Virginia does not require adjustments—categorize these as marketing or supplies.

$

Startup & Organizational Costs

Up to $5,000 in startup costs (legal, setup, training) are deductible in your first year. VA conforms to this federal rule. Excess over $5K can be amortized. Use IRS Form 4562 if required.
