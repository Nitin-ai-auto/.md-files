Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lighting, and computers used for business are deductible. Capital assets may be depreciated or deducted via Section 179. Maintain receipts and usage logs. Only the business-use portion of shared items qualifies.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Software

Routine repairs and maintenance, like lens cleaning or firmware updates, are deductible. Software subscriptions (e.g., Lightroom, Photoshop) are also eligible. Allocate shared-use software appropriately and save all receipts.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance OR
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in OR

Studio Rent & Home Office Expenses

Rent for studio or coworking spaces is deductible. Home office expenses must be calculated based on exclusive use and square footage. Use IRS Form 8829 to claim the deduction on Schedule C.

$

Utilities (Electric, Internet, Phone)

Deduct utilities like electricity, water, internet, and phone if used for business. Allocate the business-use portion. Save bills and usage records. Cell phone costs may be partly deductible if also used personally.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Car/Truck Use)

Business driving is deductible via the IRS mileage rate or actual vehicle costs. Commuting doesn’t count. Keep a mileage log or detailed receipts. Choose the method that provides the higher deduction.

$

Travel (Lodging,Transportation & Meals)

Travel to shoots or conferences—airfare, hotels, car rentals, and 50% of meals—is deductible. Deduct only the business-use portion if travel includes personal time. Document dates, destinations, and purpose.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor & Hired Assistants

Pay for second shooters, editors, or assistants is deductible. File 1099-NEC for those paid over $600. Keep payment records, contracts, and proof of business purpose. Do not mix employee wages here.

$

Training, Education & Professional Development
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education (Workshops & Courses)

Workshops and online courses that improve your current skills are deductible. Content must relate to your photography business. Maintain course outlines, registration emails, and proof of payment.

$

Professional Subscriptions & Dues

Membership fees for photography groups and trade publications are deductible. Only include the business-use portion if there is personal benefit. Keep receipts and justify the business connection.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website Cost
-------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotion

Flyers, online ads, promotional gifts, and sponsored posts are deductible. Expenses must promote your business. Retain records of platforms used and costs incurred. $25 per-client gift limit applies.

$

Website & Online Presence Costs

Deduct web hosting, domains, templates, SEO tools, and subscriptions for platforms like Squarespace. Prepaid multi-year services may be amortized. Save receipts and contracts for documentation.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Insurance premiums for liability, property, and gear coverage are deductible. Allocate if a policy is shared with personal coverage. Keep premium notices and policy details.

$

Business Licenses, Permits & Fees

Deduct business registration fees, shoot permits, and certifications required to operate legally. Includes LLC or DBA renewals and city-specific licenses. Save proof of payment and filing confirmation.

$

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props, Backdrops & Consumables

Consumables like props, backdrops, photo paper, and batteries are deductible. Expense in the year bought if inexpensive. Maintain clear separation between business and personal use and save receipts.

$

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank Charges & Merchant Fees

Bank service fees, credit card processing costs, and PayPal/Stripe transaction charges are deductible. Allocate only the business portion. Maintain statements and fee summaries.

$

Interest on Business Loans

Interest paid on business loans or equipment financing is deductible. Personal interest is not. Allocate by usage percentage. Keep amortization schedules and loan agreements as records.

$

Other (Miscellaneous & General Deductions) OR
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for other miscellaneous and general deductions OR

Pass-Through Business Income & Local Taxes

Oregon disallows the federal QBI deduction but offers reduced tax rates through Schedule OR-PTE for qualifying businesses. Local transit taxes and CAT may apply. File separately if necessary.

$

Retirement Contributions & Cultural Credits

Contributions to SEP, SIMPLE, or solo 401(k) plans reduce income. Cultural Trust donations earn tax credits. Keep detailed records and confirm eligibility for each program annually.
