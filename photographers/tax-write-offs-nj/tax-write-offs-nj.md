Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, drones, and lighting qualify under Section 179. New Jersey follows federal depreciation rules but disallows bonus depreciation. Large equipment purchases may need to be capitalized. Keep receipts and depreciation forms like IRS 4562.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Rentals

Repairs for cameras or lighting, as well as rental equipment for temporary shoots, are fully deductible. NJ treats these as ordinary business expenses. Save dated receipts and note which client/project they supported.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Studio Rent, Utilities & Office Expenses

Studio rent, internet, utilities, and office supplies are deductible. NJ conforms to federal treatment. Shared service costs must be prorated for business use. Retain contracts and bills for recordkeeping.

$

Home Office Deduction (In-home Studio)

NJ permits the federal home office deduction using either the simplified method or actual expenses. Space must be regularly and exclusively used for business. Keep square footage records and utility breakdowns.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Use (Mileage & Auto Expenses)

Use the IRS mileage rate or deduct actual expenses like gas, insurance, and maintenance. NJ accepts both methods if usage is documented. Keep logs separating personal and business use.

$

Business Travel (Airfare, Hotels & Meals)

Travel for client shoots, conferences, or training is deductible. NJ follows federal rules: meals are 50% deductible, lodging and transit are fully deductible. Document trip purpose, itinerary, and client connection.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Freelancers & Independent Contractors

Second shooters, editors, and stylists are deductible labor costs. Issue 1099-NEC for over $600 in payments. NJ requires filing NJ-W3 with 1099s. Maintain contracts and payment records.

$

Employee Wages & Payroll Taxes

Employee wages and employer-paid NJ payroll taxes are deductible. Register with NJ Division of Revenue. File NJ-927 and WR-30. Sole proprietors cannot deduct their own labor.

$

Education & Professional Development
------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Continuing Education & Training

Workshops, online classes, or courses improving photography skills are deductible. NJ matches federal rules. Save course materials and payment records. Only education related to your existing business qualifies.

$

Professional Memberships

Dues for industry associations (like PPA or APA) are deductible. NJ allows these if related to business. Social, athletic, or lobbying organizations are not eligible. Keep membership confirmations.

$

Marketing, Advertising & Website
--------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotion

Expenses for Instagram ads, print flyers, local promotions, and branded gear are deductible. NJ treats these as standard business expenses. Document ad platforms and spend allocation.

$

Website, SEO & Branding

Web design, hosting, SEO tools, and visual branding assets are deductible. NJ follows federal guidelines. One-time builds may be amortized if over the threshold. Save invoices and renewal records.

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

General liability, equipment protection, and commercial vehicle policies are deductible. NJ conforms to federal rules. Store policy statements and payment confirmations. Workers’ comp is required if you hire.

$

Business Licenses & Permits

NJ business registration, city permits, or vendor fees are deductible. NJ requires freelancers to register their trade name (DBA). Save fee receipts and registration confirmations for recordkeeping.

Props, Backdrops & Supplies
---------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Props & Studio Materials

Props, costumes, seasonal décor, and backdrops used in client shoots are deductible. NJ treats these as supply or capital expenses based on cost. Track which props are reused.

$

Consumable Supplies (USBs, Prints, Paper)

Printer ink, photo paper, packaging, batteries, and USB drives are fully deductible. NJ allows these under office supplies. Bundle small expenses and keep vendor invoices.

$

Banking, Interest & Payment Processing
--------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Loan & Credit Card Interest (Business Use)

Interest on business cards or loans is deductible federally. NJ requires an addback for most personal deductions—including loan interest. Track business-only accounts for cleaner reporting and audit safety.

$

Banking & Processor Fees

Stripe, PayPal, bank service fees, and merchant account charges are deductible. NJ treats these as standard business costs. Include processor reports and monthly account summaries.

Other (State-Specific or Niche Deductions)
------------------------------------------

New Jersey-specific and niche tax deductions

Self-Employment Tax Deduction

You can deduct 50% of SE tax on your federal return. NJ does not allow this deduction and requires you to add it back on Schedule NJ-BUS-1, Line 2a.

$

Health Insurance Premiums

Premiums for self, spouse, and dependents are deductible federally. NJ also requires an addback of this deduction—report on NJ-BUS-1, Line 2b. Keep all insurer documentation and summaries.

$

Retirement Contributions (SEP, Solo 401k)

Federal contributions are deductible, but NJ adds them back to your income. Report on NJ-BUS-1, Line 2c. Save brokerage confirmations and contribution logs.

$

Startup Costs

Deduct up to $5,000 of launch costs (like branding, legal, or training) and amortize the rest over 15 years. NJ allows the same deduction. Save dated receipts and business formation paperwork.
