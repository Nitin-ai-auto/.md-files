Equipment & Gear Essentials
---------------------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera & Equipment Purchases (Section 179 or Depreciation)

Cameras, lenses, lighting, and computer gear can be expensed via Section 179 or depreciated. Maryland caps Section 179 at $25,000 and disallows federal bonus depreciation—excess must be depreciated over time.

$

Small Tools, Software & Accessories

Memory cards, batteries, external drives, and editing software subscriptions are fully deductible in the year purchased. Maryland conforms to federal treatment for these items—track business use for each expense.

$

Depreciation Records & Usage Allocation

Maintain IRS Form 4562 and depreciation schedules for high-cost items. Maryland requires addbacks for bonus depreciation and Section 179 over $25K—use regular depreciation for state filing.

$

Business Personal Property Return

All businesses must file an annual personal property return, even if exempt. Maryland exempts businesses with total property under $20,000—self-attest exemption on the annual filing to avoid tax.

$

Studio/Office Expenses
----------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Studio Rent & Utility Bills

Rent for commercial studios and utilities (electric, internet, water) are deductible. Maryland follows federal treatment—maintain lease agreements and utility bills for records.

$

Home Office Deduction

You can claim actual expenses or use the simplified $5/sq ft method. Space must be used exclusively for business. Maryland honors this deduction as claimed on federal Form 8829.

$

Insurance Premiums for Office/Studio

Liability, property, and studio insurance are fully deductible. Maryland imposes no restrictions—keep policy statements and payment records for deduction support.

$

Business Travel, Vehicles & On-the-Go Expenses
----------------------------------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Mileage & Vehicle Use

Choose the standard mileage rate ($0.67/mile in 2024) or actual vehicle expenses. Maryland accepts both but limits heavy vehicle depreciation—spread deductions over time, not in a single year.

$

Out-of-Town Travel

Airfare, hotels, rental cars, and 50% of meals for business travel are deductible. Maryland accepts federal treatment—track business purpose, retain receipts, and separate personal from business travel.

$

Bonus Depreciation Adjustments

If bonus depreciation was used for vehicles federally, Maryland requires addbacks. Adjust your Maryland return to exclude accelerated vehicle depreciation and apply standard state depreciation instead.

$

Subcontractors, Assistants & Freelancers
----------------------------------------

Deductions for contracted labor

Contract Labor (1099-NEC)

Paying freelancers, second shooters, or editors is deductible. Issue Form 1099-NEC for payments over $600. Maryland accepts federal rules—ensure contractors are properly classified to avoid penalties.

$

Employee Wages & Payroll

If you employ staff, wages, payroll taxes, and benefits are deductible. Maryland taxes net business income after these deductions. Register for Maryland withholding and unemployment accounts if hiring employees.

$

Worker Classification & Reporting

Misclassified workers may trigger penalties. Maryland evaluates contractor status using behavioral and financial control factors—retain contracts and payment records.

$

Training, Education & Development
---------------------------------

Deductions for training & professional development

Workshops & Photography Courses

Deduct fees for training that enhances your existing skills. Maryland mirrors federal rules—only work-related courses qualify. Save enrollment confirmations and receipts.

$

Professional Memberships

Dues to photography associations like PPA or ASMP are deductible. Maryland accepts these as business expenses—exclude any portion marked for lobbying.

$

Trade Publications & Educational Travel

Subscriptions to industry magazines or sites are deductible. Travel for business education is also deductible if primary purpose is training—record purpose and keep receipts.

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for insurance & licenses

Insurance Premiums

Liability, equipment, errors & omissions, and business auto insurance premiums are deductible. Maryland follows federal rules—maintain copies of coverage documents.

$

Permits & Local Licenses

City permits, Trader’s License (for selling tangible products), and LLC/corporate filing fees (e.g. $100 formation, $300 annual report) are all deductible. Keep fee receipts.

Professional Certifications

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for supplies & consumables

Props & Decorative Items

Chairs, curtains, or outfits purchased for shoots are deductible. Maryland conforms to federal rules—expense in year purchased unless item has multi-year use.

$

Studio & Office Consumables

Memory cards, paper, cleaning kits, printer ink, and everyday office supplies are fully deductible. Track usage and keep itemized receipts.

$

Prints, Frames & Client Deliverables

Materials for albums, frames, and USB drives are deductible as cost of goods sold. If resold to clients, use Maryland resale certificate to avoid paying sales tax on purchase.

$

Sales Tax on Resold Goods

Use resale certificates when buying items for resale. Maryland taxes photography products (prints, albums) at 6%—separate product vs. service charges on invoices.

Deductions for financial expenses

Banking & Merchant Fees

Monthly bank charges, Stripe/PayPal fees, check orders, and transfer fees are deductible. Maryland recognizes these as business expenses.

$

Loan & Credit Card Interest

Other Maryland-Specific Deductions
----------------------------------

Other deductions specific to Maryland

Arts & Entertainment District Income Exemption

Income from photography created and sold in Maryland A&E Districts is exempt from state tax. File Form 502AE to subtract this from Maryland income—must be original work sold within the district.

$

Business Personal Property Tax Exemption

If total property cost is under $20,000, you are exempt from Maryland annual business personal property tax. File a return each year and self-attest to claim exemption. Above $20K, tax is deductible.
