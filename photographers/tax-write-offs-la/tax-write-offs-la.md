Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera & Equipment Purchases (Section 179 or Depreciation)

You can deduct the full cost of gear like cameras and lighting using Section 179 or depreciation. Louisiana conforms to federal rules and allows 100% bonus depreciation starting 2025. Use IRS Form 4562 and retain receipts for audit protection.

$

Small Equipment & Supplies

Items under $2,500 like tripods, batteries, and SD cards can be expensed immediately under the federal de minimis rule. Louisiana mirrors this treatment with no additional limits. Keep receipts and documentation of business use for substantiation.

$

Sales Tax & Use Tax on Equipment

Louisiana charges 5% state sales tax (plus parish surtax) on in-state gear purchases. If you buy out-of-state without tax, you must self-remit Louisiana use tax. File Form R-1035. Paid tax becomes part of your deductible federal cost.

$

Business Personal Property Tax

Louisiana has no statewide property tax, but parishes may assess tax on business equipment. Any property tax you pay is deductible federally. Retain assessment notices and proof of payment from your parish for documentation.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance LA
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in LA

Studio Rent & Utilities

Rent for a studio or co-working space used in your business is fully deductible. Utilities like electricity, water, and internet are ordinary expenses. Louisiana follows federal treatment. Commercial rent is not subject to Louisiana sales tax.

$

Home Office Deduction

You can deduct part of your home expenses if used exclusively and regularly for business. Use IRS Form 8829 for the regular method or the simplified option ($5/sq ft). Louisiana mirrors federal treatment with no separate form or limits.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Business Travel & Lodging

Travel costs like airfare, hotels, Ubers, and baggage fees for shoots or conferences are deductible. Only the business portion is allowed if mixed with personal travel. Louisiana honors federal rules. In-state travel taxes are included in deductible costs.

$

Vehicle Expenses (Mileage or Actual)

Use the standard mileage rate ($0.67 in 2024) or deduct actual costs like gas, insurance, and depreciation based on business use. Louisiana accepts both methods. Keep mileage logs and receipts. Use IRS Form 4562 for vehicle depreciation.

$

Parking Fees, Tolls & Local Transport

Parking and tolls during business trips are deductible even when using mileage rate. Business-related Uber, Lyft, or public transit costs are also deductible. Louisiana mirrors federal rules with no extra filings required.

$

Luxury Vehicle & SUV Write-Offs

SUVs over 6,000 lbs GVWR may qualify for full expensing via Section 179 (up to $28,900 in 2024). Lighter vehicles have first-year caps (~$20,200). Louisiana allows bonus depreciation starting in 2025. Use Form 4562 to claim vehicle depreciation.

$

Commuting vs. Business Miles

Driving from home to a regular workplace (like your studio) is commuting and not deductible. Trips to client shoots, temporary locations, or meetings are business miles. Document each trip with date, miles, and purpose. Applies for both federal and Louisiana.

$

Sales Tax on Rentals

When renting vehicles or booking hotels in Louisiana, the sales tax paid is included in the deductible cost. No exemptions exist for photographers. Local occupancy and rental taxes also count toward your business travel expense total.

$

Contracted Labor
----------------

Deductions for contracted labor

Independent Contractors & Second Shooters

Payments to second shooters, editors, makeup artists, or assistants are deductible under Contract Labor. Issue Form 1099-NEC for payments of $600 or more. Louisiana participates in the IRS Combined Filing Program—no separate state 1099 form required.

$

W-9 Collection & Recordkeeping

Always collect a completed Form W-9 before paying contractors. This ensures you have their legal name and taxpayer ID for 1099 filing. Louisiana follows federal documentation rules. Maintain payment records and proof of services for at least three years.

$

Out-of-State Contractors

If you hire out-of-state freelancers for in-state jobs, they may owe Louisiana income tax. While you can deduct the payment, advise contractors about their Louisiana tax obligations. This applies mostly to large or ongoing engagements.

$

Employee Wages & Payroll Taxes

If you hire W-2 employees (e.g., part-time studio assistant), their wages and employer payroll taxes are deductible. Louisiana requires a state withholding tax account. Also deduct any state unemployment insurance contributions paid for employee wages.

$

Worker Classification Rules

Misclassifying employees as contractors can trigger penalties. Use IRS guidelines on behavioral and financial control. Louisiana defers to federal classification rules but enforces proper worker designation for withholding and unemployment coverage.

$

Training & Professional Development
-----------------------------------

Deductions for training & professional development

Workshops, Courses & Conferences

Expenses for education that improves your photography skills—like workshops, seminars, or conferences—are deductible. Travel costs are deducted separately. Louisiana conforms to federal rules. In-person courses may include sales tax, which becomes part of the deduction.

$

Online Courses & Tutorials

Online classes, downloadable tutorials, and video training are deductible if related to your business. Louisiana may tax some digital products starting in 2025. Any sales tax paid is part of your expense. Retain proof of purchase and course content.

$

Coaching, Mentoring & One-on-One Training

Fees paid to mentors or for private coaching to improve your skills are deductible. Louisiana mirrors federal rules. Be sure to document the purpose and date of each session. Payment records should be retained for audit support.

$

Books, Magazines & Educational Subscriptions

Instructional books, photography magazines, and online subscriptions are deductible. Louisiana follows federal treatment. If sales tax is charged, it’s included in your deductible cost. Keep receipts and subscription invoices.

$

Professional Memberships

Membership dues for organizations like PPA or local photographer guilds are deductible if they enhance your business skills or provide networking. Louisiana has no limits or reporting requirements. Store confirmation emails and payment records for reference.

$

Marketing & Advertising
-----------------------

Deductions for marketing & advertising

Online Ads & Social Media Promotions

Ads on Google, Instagram, Facebook, and directories are fully deductible. Louisiana does not tax ad services. Document your ad spend and keep receipts or digital invoices to support the expense in case of an audit.

$

Print Advertising & Flyers

Print ads, brochures, and mailers are deductible. Design and printing costs are included. Louisiana applies sales tax to printed materials. That tax becomes part of your deductible cost. Keep vendor invoices and samples on file.

$

Website, Hosting & Domain Costs

Fees for website design, hosting, and domain registration are deductible. Louisiana does not tax web services. If you sell products online, check for use tax obligations on goods shipped to Louisiana clients.

$

SEO, Branding & Marketing Consultants

Fees for SEO, logo design, or marketing consultants are deductible. Louisiana treats these as nontaxable professional services. Retain service contracts, invoices, and proof of payment for your tax records.

$

Promotional Items & Swag

Branded gifts like USBs, prints, or apparel are deductible advertising costs. Louisiana sales tax applies if purchased in-state. Out-of-state purchases may trigger use tax. Include tax paid as part of the deductible total cost.

$

Portfolio Samples & Bridal Shows

Sample albums, trade show materials, and expo booth fees are deductible. Louisiana applies sales tax to printed samples and in-state supplies. Maintain all receipts and registration confirmations for accurate deduction.

$

Insurance & Licenses
--------------------

Deductions for insurance & licenses

Business Insurance Premiums

Business insurance—liability, property, or auto (proportionate to business use)—is fully deductible. Louisiana imposes no sales tax on insurance premiums. Deduct on your federal return; Louisiana accepts the federal treatment automatically.

$

Licenses, Permits & Certifications

Occupational licenses, shoot permits, and LLC fees are deductible. Most Louisiana parishes require an annual occupational license. Permit fees tied to specific shoots are also deductible. Keep copies of applications and payment confirmations.

$

Supplies & Consumables
----------------------

Deductions for supplies & consumables

Props, Backdrops & Disposable Materials

Props, fabrics, and consumables used in shoots are deductible. Louisiana charges sales tax on in-state purchases; that tax becomes part of your deductible cost. Use tax applies to untaxed online or out-of-state buys.

$

Office & Digital Supplies

Items like printer paper, shipping supplies, presets, and software subscriptions are deductible. Louisiana taxes digital goods starting 2025. Any sales tax paid becomes part of the deduction. Retain receipts and usage records.

$

Financial Expenses
------------------

Deductions for financial expenses

Bank Fees & Payment Processor Charges

Bank account fees, ATM fees, and credit card processing fees from services like Stripe and PayPal are deductible. Louisiana does not require separate reporting. Save monthly statements and transaction records to document business charges.

$

Loan & Credit Card Interest

Interest on business loans or credit cards is deductible if related to your business. Louisiana conforms to federal rules. Separate personal from business use and keep lender statements as year-end documentation.

$

Other Deductions & Credits
--------------------------

Deductions for other deductions & credits

Qualified Business Income Deduction

The federal QBI deduction allows 20% off qualified net profit. Louisiana does not conform—it taxes your full business profit since QBI is deducted after federal AGI. No state form or adjustment is needed.

$

Sales Tax on Deliverables

Louisiana taxes digital and physical photo deliverables starting 2025. If you provide a USB or gallery, the entire charge may be taxable. Register to collect and remit Louisiana sales tax through the state or parish systems.

$

Cultural District Sales Tax Exemption

Sales of original, one-of-a-kind art in certified Cultural Districts are exempt from Louisiana sales tax. Applies to signed, limited-edition photo art only. Register with the state and retain documentation and certificates of authenticity.
