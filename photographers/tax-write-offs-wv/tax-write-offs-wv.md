Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera & Equipment Purchases

You can deduct full costs of gear like cameras, lenses, and computers using Section 179 or depreciate over time. WV conforms to federal rules. Track business use and file IRS Form 4562 if depreciating assets.

$

Equipment Repairs & Maintenance

Routine repair costs such as cleaning, part replacement, or maintenance of gear are fully deductible. This includes calibration services, sensor cleanings, or minor fixes. Keep all receipts to substantiate your expense.

$

Equipment Rentals

Short-term gear rentals (lighting, cameras) are 100% deductible as business expenses. For WV, rental sales tax paid is part of the deduction. Lease-to-own contracts may need to be capitalized and depreciated.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance WV
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in WV

Studio Rent & Utilities

Rent for a studio or co-working space is fully deductible. So are utilities like electricity, internet, or water if used solely for business. Shared space expenses must be prorated.

$

Home Office Deduction

If you work from a home office used exclusively for business, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, and insurance. Choose simplified ($5/sq ft) or actual expense method. File IRS Form 8829 for actual costs.

$

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for general liability, property, or equipment insurance are fully deductible. This includes coverage for studio space, gear, and E&O policies. WV allows full deduction—no separate state limits apply.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Use

Use the standard mileage rate (70¢ per mile in 2025) or deduct actual vehicle costs used for business. Maintain a mileage log with dates, miles, and purpose. Commuting is not deductible.

$

Business Travel

Travel for shoots, conferences, or workshops is deductible. Includes airfare, hotels, car rentals, and 50% of business-related meals. Trips must be primarily for business. Save receipts and record the trip purpose.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Payments to Contractors & Freelancers

Paying second shooters, editors, or assistants is fully deductible. If you pay anyone $600+ in a year, issue them a Form 1099-NEC. Keep W-9s, invoices, and proof of payments.

$

Employee Wages

If you hire employees, wages and employer payroll taxes are deductible. This includes Social Security, Medicare, and unemployment contributions. You will need to manage payroll filings and tax withholdings.

$

----------------------------------------------

Deductions for training, education & professional development

Workshops & Courses

Educational expenses to improve photography or business skills are deductible. This includes workshops, online courses, books, certifications, and industry conference fees. The training must relate to your current business.

$

Professional Subscriptions & Dues

Memberships in photography associations or subscriptions to trade publications are deductible. This includes platforms for continuing education or professional development like online learning portals or guild memberships.

Marketing, Advertising & Website Cost
-------------------------------------

Deductions for marketing, advertising & website cost

Advertising & Promotion

All advertising expenses to attract clients are deductible. This includes Facebook ads, Google Ads, flyers, brochures, sponsorships, and branded giveaways. Keep receipts or contracts showing the business intent behind promotional spend.

$

Website Design & Hosting

Costs to build or maintain your business website are fully deductible. This includes domain names, hosting, templates, SEO help, and portfolio platform subscriptions. Freelancers hired to update content are also deductible under advertising or contract labor.

$

Marketing Materials & Branding

Logo design, business cards, printed mailers, and signage are marketing expenses. Branded items like client welcome kits or promotional gifts count, too. Clothing with your business logo is deductible if used solely for promotional purposes.

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for liability, equipment, or errors & omissions insurance are deductible. Coverage for studio property or business interruption is also included. Only business-related policies count—personal home or life insurance doesn’t qualify.

$

Licenses & Permits

You can deduct fees for business licenses, permits, or certificates needed to operate legally. This includes state registration fees, DBA filings, or photography location permits. Keep official documents and receipts for proof.

$

Professional Certifications

Fees for certifications like drone licensing or professional photography credentials are deductible. This also includes prep course fees or exams directly tied to your photography business operations. Maintain all invoices or confirmation emails.

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for props, backdrops & consumable supplies

Shoot Props & Backdrops

Costs for props, backdrops, reflectors, and set materials used in client sessions are fully deductible. Items must be purchased for business purposes. Keep receipts and avoid mixing with personal-use items.

$

Consumable Office Supplies

Business-use items like printer ink, paper, pens, and folders are deductible in full. These are generally expensed in the year purchased. Maintain records to support purchases, especially when items are shared with personal use.

$

Photo & Shipping Supplies

Expenses for memory cards, batteries, external drives, USBs, and packaging for client deliveries are deductible. Include shipping labels, boxes, and mailing costs. Deduct the full business-use portion with receipts for verification.

$

Client Print Production

The cost of prints, photo books, or canvases produced for clients is deductible. Whether ordered through a lab or printed in-house, it qualifies as a supply or cost of goods sold.

Bank Fees, Loan Interest & Financial Expenses
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for bank fees, loan interest & financial expenses

Bank & Merchant Fees

Monthly business bank fees, ATM charges, check ordering, and payment processing fees (PayPal, Stripe, Square) are deductible. These routine charges reduce your net business income. Save statements showing dates and fee amounts.

$

Business Loan Interest

Interest on loans or credit lines used for business purchases (e.g., equipment, software) is deductible. Only the interest portion—not principal—is expensed. Keep loan agreements and payment statements showing the business-use connection.

$

Credit Card & Financing Costs

Annual fees and interest from business credit cards are deductible if used for business purchases. Allocate interest based on the business-use percentage. Retain credit card statements and track which purchases relate to your work.

$

Financial Services & Advice

Fees for accountants, bookkeepers, and financial advisors offering business-related services are fully deductible. This includes consultation on taxes, budgeting, and setup. Save invoices or emails describing services rendered for accurate expense tracking.

Other (Miscellaneous & General Deductions) WV
---------------------------------------------

Deductions for other miscellaneous and general deductions WV

Business Meals

You can deduct 50% of meal costs during business travel or client meetings. Meals must be directly related to business. Keep itemized receipts and note the purpose (e.g., client pitch or event coverage meal).

$

Phone & Internet

Deduct the business-use portion of your cell phone or internet bill. If shared with personal use, estimate and document the percentage used for work (e.g., 70%). Fully deductible if it’s a dedicated business line.

$

Professional Dues & Subscriptions

Fees for memberships in industry organizations (e.g., PPA), photography directories, or online portfolios (Pixieset, SmugMug) are deductible. Subscriptions to trade journals, learning platforms, or photography blogs also count. Maintain receipts or digital invoices.

$

Legal & Professional Services

Attorney or CPA fees related to contracts, trademarks, or tax filing are deductible. This includes preparing Schedule C, registering a business, or reviewing client agreements. Save engagement letters, invoices, or receipts for documentation.

$

Filing & Admin Fees

Deduct WV-specific fees like the $30 Business Registration Certificate or annual LLC reports. Also include other business registrations like a DBA. Keep documentation for each filing fee or payment made to WV agencies.
