Equipment And Gear
------------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Business Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lenses, lighting gear, and computers used 100% for photography are deductible using Section 179 or depreciation. Rhode Island allows full federal Section 179 but disallows bonus depreciation. Maintain separate RI depreciation schedules.

$

Sales Tax on Equipment Purchases

Rhode Island’s 7% sales tax on gear purchases is included in the asset cost. This total is deductible under Section 179 or depreciation. Out-of-state or untaxed online purchases require you to pay RI use tax, which is also deductible.

$

Depreciation Recapture & Asset Disposals

If you sell depreciated equipment, RI requires recalculating gain/loss using its state-specific depreciation basis. Adjust income accordingly. Maintain detailed asset records, depreciation schedules, and sale documents.

$

Repair & Maintenance of Gear

Routine camera or lighting repairs and maintenance services are deductible as operating expenses. RI follows federal treatment. These are not capitalized unless part of a major equipment overhaul.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses

Studio or Home Office Rent

Rent for studio space or a qualified home office is deductible. RI conforms to federal rules. Deduct based on business-use percentage. Maintain lease agreements and utility cost breakdowns.

$

Utilities (Electricity, Internet, Phone)

Electricity, water, internet, and phone bills for studio or home office use are deductible in proportion to business usage. RI accepts these under federal Schedule C with no modifications.

$

Studio Insurance (Property & Casualty)

Premiums for business-use liability, property, or renter’s insurance are deductible. RI imposes no special limits. Only business-related insurance qualifies, and you must separate from personal coverage.

$

Real Estate & Personal Property Tax

Local tangible personal property tax on business equipment is deductible. RI municipalities may exempt low-value property. Include all documented property taxes on Schedule C as valid expenses.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Local Transportation)

Deduct driving to shoots or errands using actual expenses or IRS mileage. RI follows federal rules and rates. Maintain a mileage log or receipts. Commuting from home to studio is not deductible unless home is the main office.

$

Business Travel (Out-of-Town)

Airfare, lodging, and 50% of meals for business travel are deductible. RI follows IRS guidelines, including per diem options. Save receipts and note business purpose. Mixed personal/business trips must be allocated properly.

$

Parking, Tolls & Ride Services

Expenses like parking at venues, tolls, and business-use Uber/Lyft rides are deductible. RI accepts these if they meet IRS “ordinary and necessary” criteria. Keep receipts or app records.

$

Travel Meals & Incidental Costs

50% of business meals and tips while traveling are deductible. RI respects this federal limitation. Meals must have a clear business connection. Document attendees and purpose on receipts.

$

Contracted Labor & Assistants
-----------------------------

Deductions for contracted labor

Freelance Assistants and Second Shooters

Paying over $600 to freelancers requires a 1099-NEC. RI follows federal rules. Document names, roles, and amounts paid. No extra RI filings, but maintain proof in case of audit.

$

Outsourced Services (Editing, Printing, etc.)

Paying labs, editors, or web designers counts as deductible contract services. RI conforms with federal treatment. Use a resale certificate for any deliverables resold to clients to avoid duplicate tax.

$

Client-Facing Subcontractor Costs

Subcontractor charges embedded in client packages are deductible. RI taxes the full sale if tangible goods are included, but contractor costs remain deductible.

$

Non-1099 Contractor Payments

Even if you pay under $600, those freelancer payments are deductible. RI allows all ordinary and necessary contract labor deductions. Keep records even without issuing 1099s.

$

Training & Professional Development
-----------------------------------

Deductions for training & professional development

Continuing Education & Workshops

Fees for photography workshops and business training related to your current trade are deductible. RI fully conforms with federal rules. These must improve existing skills, not train you for a new profession.

$

Professional Memberships & Subscriptions

Dues to PPA or local guilds, subscriptions to industry magazines or cloud services are deductible. RI recognizes these costs when related to business operations. Exclude any portion related to personal use.

$

Certification & Licensing Programs

Costs for obtaining credentials (like drone certification) are deductible if tied to your current business. RI allows these under ordinary business expense rules.

$

Training Travel & Materials

Travel and material costs related to education (like lodging for a seminar or books for a class) are deductible. RI conforms to federal treatment—ensure these are documented and related to your current business.

$

Marketing & Advertising
-----------------------

Deductions for marketing & advertising

Digital Advertising (Social, Google Ads, etc.)

Online ads and promos are fully deductible. RI has no additional rules and allows these costs under ordinary expenses. Document spend and targeting.

$

Print Marketing & Materials

Flyers, business cards, and branded promo materials are deductible. RI includes sales tax in the cost. Use actual receipts and invoices for documentation.

$

Website, SEO & Hosting Fees

Expenses for building and maintaining your business website—including design, hosting, and SEO—are deductible. RI does not tax digital services and recognizes these as marketing costs.

$

Client Meals & Hospitality

Client meetings over meals are 50% deductible. RI follows federal limits and restrictions on entertainment expenses. Meals must be ordinary, necessary, and properly documented.

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for supplies & consumables

Props, Backdrops, and Set Materials

Backdrops, props, and themed décor used in photo sessions are deductible. RI treats these as supplies. Sales tax or use tax on purchases adds to your deductible cost.

$

Consumable Supplies

Printer ink, shipping supplies, batteries, and other day-to-day items are deductible. RI expects consistency with the federal treatment. Track business-use allocation carefully.

$

Printing, Albums, and Deliverables

Client deliverables like albums and prints are deductible. Use a resale certificate to avoid double taxation. RI allows deduction of full costs incurred to produce these items.

$

Sales Tax & Use Tax Compliance

RI requires use tax on out-of-state purchases without sales tax. This tax becomes part of your expense if the item is for business use. Use resale certificates where applicable.

$

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for insurance & licenses

Business Liability Insurance

Premiums for general liability, professional indemnity, and equipment coverage specific to your business are deductible. RI fully allows these expenses. Only policies directly tied to business risks or operations qualify.

$

Licenses, Permits & Fees

Fees for Rhode Island business registration, local licenses, and permits (e.g., state park shooting permits) are deductible. RI allows full deduction for ordinary business permits and registrations.

$

Sales Tax Permit & Resale Certificates

The $10 annual RI sales tax permit and any fees related to obtaining resale certificates are deductible. RI expects proper documentation and compliance with state tax rules.

$

Fire Inspection & Compliance Costs

Fees for municipal inspections, such as fire code compliance for a studio space, are deductible. RI considers these valid operating expenses. Keep receipts and compliance documents for records.

$

Other (Niche or Rhode Island-Specific Deductions)
-------------------------------------------------

Deductions for financial expenses

Arts District Income Tax Exemption

If certified in an Arts District, income from original photo art sold in-district may be exempt from RI income tax. This does not apply to client work like weddings. Certification and documentation required.

$

Statewide Sales-Tax Exemption for Fine Art

RI exempts sales of fine art, including photography, from sales tax statewide. You must register for an exemption certificate. This applies only to original or limited edition artwork, not commercial shoots.

$

Municipal Taxes on Business Property

City-assessed tangible personal property tax is deductible as a business expense. RI follows federal treatment. Keep assessor bills and payment receipts.

$

Loan Interest, Financial Services & Bank Fees

Interest on business loans, credit card fees, and bank charges are deductible. RI accepts all such expenses when related to business. Maintain supporting documentation for each account or service.
