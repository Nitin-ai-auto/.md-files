Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Cameras, lighting, tripods, and computers used for business are deductible. Capital assets may be depreciated or expensed under Section 179. Keep receipts and allocate based on business use.

$

Repairs, Maintenance & Software

Routine maintenance and repairs (e.g., lens cleaning or computer upgrades) are deductible. Depreciate major purchases if not expensed. Maintain documentation of expenses and depreciation schedules.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance CO
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in CO

Studio Rent & Home Office Expenses

Rent for studios, offices, or short-term rentals used for business is fully deductible. Include utilities and insurance for the space. Deduct only the business-use portion if shared.

$

Utilities (Electric, Internet, Phone)

Deduct utilities like electricity, water, internet, and phone if used for business. Allocate the business-use portion. Save bills and usage records. Cell phone costs may be partly deductible if also used personally.

$

Home Office (Business Use of Home)

Deduct a portion of home rent, mortgage interest, utilities, and repairs if an area is used exclusively for business. Use actual expenses or the simplified IRS method.

$

Business Insurance & Licenses

Business-related insurance premiums and required license or permit fees are deductible. Includes liability insurance, equipment insurance, and memberships like PPA.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Driving for Business)

Deduct business mileage using the IRS standard rate or actual expenses. Keep logs and receipts. Choose the method offering the higher deduction.

$

Business Travel (Airfare, Lodging & Meals)

Airfare, lodging, and 50% of business meals while traveling are deductible. Keep all receipts and document the business purpose of the trip.

$

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor (Freelance Assistants & Second Shooters)

Payments to second shooters, editors, and assistants are deductible. Issue 1099-NEC if payments exceed $600. Maintain contracts and payment records.

Other (Colorado-Specific Deductions or Credits)
-----------------------------------------------

Deductions for Colorado-specific credits and niche tax benefits

Colorado Business Personal Property Tax Credit

You can claim a refundable credit for county personal property taxes paid on business equipment (up to $18,000 in asset value). File with your state return and attach your tax receipts.

$

Enterprise Zone Tax Credits

If operating in a Colorado Enterprise Zone, claim credits like 3% on equipment purchases or $1,100 per new job. Must pre-certify each year and file Form DR 1366 with proof.

$

Innovative Motor Vehicle Credit (EV Credit)

Receive up to a $5,000 tax credit for purchasing a new electric or plug-in hybrid vehicle registered in Colorado. Credit applies regardless of business/personal use split and can carry forward.
