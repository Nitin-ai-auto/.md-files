Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Equipment Purchases & Depreciation

Photography gear like cameras, drones, and editing computers are deductible assets. SC follows federal Section 179 and MACRS but disallows bonus depreciation. Track receipts, allocate business-use only, and file depreciation schedules with your return.

$

Equipment Repairs & Rentals

Repair costs and rental fees for gear are fully deductible. Save invoices and rental agreements. Sales tax on rentals is deductible. Report these as ordinary expenses on your South Carolina Schedule C.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance
-----------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in South Carolina

Studio Rent & Home Office Expenses

Studio rent is deductible in full. Home office deductions follow federal rules using actual costs or the simplified method. SC accepts Form 8829 figures from Schedule C with no extra forms required.

$

Office Utilities & Property Insurance

Deduct utilities like internet, phone, and insurance based on business-use percentage. SC mirrors federal treatment—if claimed federally, they’re accepted on your SC return with no separate adjustment.

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Vehicle Expenses (Car/Truck Use)

Use the federal mileage rate or actual expenses to deduct local business driving. Maintain detailed logs. SC follows IRS rules and allows proportional deduction for business-use vehicle property taxes.

$

Business Travel (Lodging, Transportation & Meals)

Out-of-town travel costs—airfare, hotels, transport, and 50% of meals—are deductible. SC follows federal rules with no state-specific limits. Maintain records showing business purpose.

Subcontractors & Assistants
---------------------------

Deductions for subcontractors and assistants

Contract Labor & Assistants

Pay for second shooters or editors is deductible. Issue 1099-NEC if paid ≥ $600. SC matches federal rules with no separate filing required. Keep contractor W-9s and service documentation.

$

Payroll-Related Fees & Licensing

If you hit employee thresholds, SC may require workers’ comp coverage. Fees for labor-related registrations or insurance are deductible. Most freelancers remain under this threshold, but document everything.

Professional Dues & Subscriptions

Business Insurance, Licensing & Permits
---------------------------------------

Deductions for business insurance, licensing & permits

Insurance Premiums

Business liability, gear, studio, and cyber insurance premiums are deductible. SC conforms to federal treatment—life insurance with you as beneficiary is not deductible. Retain all policy documents.

Business Licenses & Photography Permits

Local business licenses, SC Retail License, and location-specific shoot permits are deductible. SC requires sales tax collection on physical product sales. License fees and use taxes are deductible as business costs.

Other (Miscellaneous & State-Specific)
--------------------------------------

Deductions for other niche or South Carolina-specific deductions

Health Insurance (Personal Deduction)

Self-employed health insurance premiums are deductible above-the-line on federal returns. SC accepts this deduction via federal AGI.

$

Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

SC does not allow the federal QBI 20% deduction. You must add it back when calculating SC income. Plan for higher taxable income on your state return.

$

Startup Costs & Entity Licensing Fees

Up to $5,000 in startup expenses are deductible. LLC formation and licensing fees are deductible. SC conforms to federal treatment with no additional filing burden.

$

Hobby Loss Consideration

Your photography must be a business, not a hobby. If deemed a hobby, SC (like the IRS) disallows all deductions. Maintain records, pursue profit, and use separate accounts.
