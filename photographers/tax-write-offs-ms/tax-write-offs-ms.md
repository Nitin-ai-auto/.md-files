Equipment & Gear
----------------

Deductions for photography equipment and related gear

Camera & Equipment Purchases

You can deduct gear like cameras, lenses, and computers by depreciating over 5–7 years or using Section 179 or bonus depreciation. Mississippi conforms fully to federal rules and allows 100% first-year expensing. Use IRS Form 4562.

$

Small Equipment & Supplies

Items like tripods, memory cards, and accessories under $2,500 can be expensed immediately using the federal de minimis rule. Mississippi allows the same treatment. Keep detailed purchase records and business-use notes for each item.

$

Sales Tax & Use Tax on Equipment

Mississippi charges 7% sales tax on photography gear bought in-state. Out-of-state or online purchases without sales tax require you to remit use tax. Sales or use tax paid becomes part of your deductible equipment cost.

$

Business Personal Property Depreciation

Mississippi does not require a separate depreciation schedule. Report deductions on your federal return and they carry through to your state return. If you later sell equipment, depreciation recapture applies. Keep inventory logs and sales records.

$

Studio/Office Rent, Utilities & Insurance MS
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for studio and office expenses in MS

Studio Rent & Utilities

Rent for a photography studio or office is fully deductible. Utilities like electricity, water, and internet are included. Mississippi does not tax real property leases. Commercial utility costs are deductible, including any sales tax paid.

$

Home Office Deduction

If you work from home, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, insurance, and repairs based on exclusive business use. Use Form 8829 or the simplified $5/sq ft method. Mississippi follows federal treatment with no state-level adjustments.

$

Phone & Internet Use (Business Portion)

A separate business phone or internet line is 100% deductible. If using personal service, deduct only the business-use portion. Mississippi conforms to federal treatment. Maintain call logs or allocation records to support business use percentages.

$

Travel & Transportation
-----------------------

Deductions for travel and transportation expenses

Business Travel & Lodging

Travel costs for photo shoots, conferences, or meetings—like airfare, hotels, and car rentals—are deductible. Mississippi follows federal rules, including the 50% meal limit and no entertainment deduction. Keep receipts and document the business purpose.

$

Vehicle Expenses (Mileage or Actual)

Use the IRS standard mileage rate ($0.70 in 2025) or deduct actual vehicle costs. Mississippi conforms to federal depreciation and Section 179 rules. Commuting is not deductible—only business miles count. Maintain a mileage log for audit support.

$

Parking Fees, Tolls & Local Transport

Parking, tolls, and ride-share fares are deductible if business-related. Mississippi does not tax Uber or taxi services. If travel is part of a shoot or event, include these costs with your business transportation expenses.

$

Sales Tax on Gas & Rentals

Gas costs (including fuel tax) are deductible under actual expense method. Rental cars used for business may have rental taxes—those are deductible too. Mississippi accepts the full cost as long as it aligns with federal reporting.

$

Contracted Labor
----------------

Deductions for contracted labor

Independent Contractors & Freelancers

Payments to second shooters, editors, or assistants are fully deductible as contract labor. Issue Form 1099-NEC for any non-corporate payee earning $600 or more. Mississippi follows federal rules and does not require separate contractor withholding.

$

Wages & Payroll Taxes for Employees

If you hire W-2 employees, wages, employer payroll taxes, and benefits are deductible. Mississippi requires registration for withholding and unemployment tax accounts. All employment-related expenses follow federal treatment and flow to your state return.

$

Worker Classification & 1099 Filing

Classify helpers correctly—contractors operate independently, employees are under your control. Misclassification can trigger penalties. Keep W-9s, invoices, and payment records. Mississippi DOR accepts federal 1099-NEC filings and may cross-reference during audits.

$

Training & Professional Development
-----------------------------------

Deductions for training & professional development

Workshops, Courses & Seminars

Fees for photography workshops, lighting classes, or marketing seminars that enhance your skills are deductible. Mississippi follows federal rules. Education must relate to your current trade. Retain course details and payment receipts for records.

$

Online Training & Tutorials

Online classes, video courses, or downloadable content that improves your photography business are deductible. Mississippi does not tax educational services. Include any sales tax paid on related materials in the total deduction amount.

$

Mentorships & Portfolio Reviews

Payments for portfolio reviews, coaching, or one-on-one mentorships are deductible if skill-related. Mississippi accepts this as an ordinary business expense. Keep documentation showing relevance to your current photography services.

$

Books, Subscriptions & Industry Resources

Trade books, magazines, and subscriptions like PPA or educational platforms are deductible. Mississippi conforms to federal treatment. Sales tax on in-state purchases becomes part of the deductible cost.

$

Marketing & Advertising
-----------------------

Deductions for marketing & advertising

Online Ads & Promotions

Ads on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Google are fully deductible. Mississippi follows federal treatment. Online ad services are not subject to Mississippi sales tax. Retain ad receipts or digital billing records for documentation.

$

Print Advertising & Business Cards

Printed flyers, brochures, business cards, and print ads are deductible. Mississippi applies sales tax to printing services; the tax is included in your deductible cost. Keep vendor invoices and samples of printed materials for records.

$

Website, Hosting & Domain Services

Website setup, hosting fees, domain name renewals, and portfolio site subscriptions are deductible. Mississippi generally does not tax these digital services. Include receipts and any applicable sales tax paid in your deduction.

$

SEO, Branding & Promotional Services

Logo design, branding, SEO services, and paid directory listings are deductible. Mississippi treats these as ordinary business expenses. Most professional service fees are not taxed. Maintain contracts and payment records.

$

Promotional Items & Client Gifts

Branded items like USBs, thank-you cards, and swag are deductible. Mississippi taxes these goods at 7%; the tax is part of your expense. Deduct up to $25 per client for gifts unless branded—then it's 100% advertising.

$

Event Participation & Portfolio Samples

Trade show fees, bridal expo booths, and display prints are deductible. Printing is taxable in Mississippi—include sales tax in the cost. Keep registration confirmations, supply receipts, and documentation of business use.

Insurance & Licenses
--------------------

Deductions for insurance & licenses

Business Insurance Premiums

Premiums for general liability, gear insurance, studio property insurance, or business-use vehicle coverage are fully deductible. Mississippi does not tax insurance premiums. Deduct expenses on your federal return and Mississippi will conform automatically.

$

Licenses, Permits & Certification Fees

Business license fees, local privilege taxes, and permits for shoots are deductible. Mississippi allows deductions for state and local business fees except for income tax. Keep copies of paid permits, business license renewals, and related receipts.

$

Professional Memberships & Compliance Costs

Dues paid to photography associations and chambers of commerce are deductible. PCI compliance fees from payment processors also qualify. Mississippi follows federal treatment. Document payments with confirmation emails or bank statements.

$

Props, Backdrops & Consumable Supplies
--------------------------------------

Deductions for supplies & consumables

Set Props, Decor & Shoot Materials

Items like fabrics, props, flowers, and decor used in photo shoots are deductible. Mississippi charges 7% sales tax on purchases, which becomes part of the deductible cost. Keep all receipts and categorize purchases based on shoot use.

$

Office Supplies & Consumables

Printer ink, paper, pens, packaging, and shipping supplies are deductible. Mississippi follows federal treatment. Sales tax paid on in-state purchases is included in the deductible cost. Maintain purchase records and label for business use.

$

Stock Media & Digital Assets

Expenses for stock music, digital backgrounds, or downloadable overlays used in editing are deductible. Mississippi treats digital goods as taxable—include sales tax in your deduction. Keep invoices and licensing confirmations for each digital asset.

$

Financial Expenses
------------------

Deductions for financial expenses

Business Bank Charges & Merchant Fees

Monthly account fees, wire transfer charges, and card processing fees from Stripe, PayPal, or Square are deductible. Mississippi follows federal treatment. These services are not subject to sales tax and are included as business expenses.

$

Loan Interest & Credit Card Charges

Interest paid on loans or credit cards used for business is deductible. Mississippi allows full deduction of interest on business debt. Principal is not deductible. Keep amortization schedules or statements showing interest amounts.

$

Accountant & Legal Fees (Business Use)

Fees for tax prep, bookkeeping, and legal contract review are deductible. Mississippi treats these as ordinary expenses. Keep clear records showing business use. If mixed with personal services, deduct only the business-use portion.

$

Other (Miscellaneous & Mississippi-Specific)
--------------------------------------------

Deductions for other deductions & credits

Self-Employment Tax Adjustment

You may deduct 50% of your federal self-employment tax from Mississippi income, matching federal treatment. Report this on Form 80-105. Attach Schedule SE for verification. This adjustment lowers your state taxable income directly.

$

Health Insurance (Self-Employed)

Premiums for medical, dental, and long-term care insurance (for you, spouse, dependents) are deductible above the line federally and also in Mississippi. Use the same amount claimed on your federal return for your state adjustment.

$

Retirement Contributions (SEP, SIMPLE, Solo 401k)

Contributions to self-employed retirement plans are deductible federally and in Mississippi. No state-specific cap. Deduct the same amount as on your federal return. Retain plan statements or worksheets in case of audit.

$

Sales Tax Exclusion for Income

Mississippi sales tax collected from clients is not counted as income. Exclude it from gross revenue. Only include the service/product price in taxable income. Don’t deduct sales tax—just don’t include it in the first place.

$

Home Office Setup Costs

Equipment like desks, chairs, and cabinets under $2,500 can be expensed immediately. Mississippi conforms to the federal de minimis rule. Larger items should be depreciated. Maintain purchase records and business-use documentation.
